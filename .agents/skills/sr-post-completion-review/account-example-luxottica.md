## Review posture

Do not mark a Luxottica SR clean because it is closed in Vixxo's system. The review standard is whether the closeout evidence is strong enough for Luxottica's customer workflow and whether any inspection-derived work has a visible owner.

High-signal Luxottica questions:

1. Does the resolution note satisfy the customer-facing closure requirement, or is it only an internal "work complete" note?
2. Did inspection notes identify repair, quote, parts, return visit, or project scope that has no child SR / quote / owner?
3. Is the SR sitting in invoice workflow while the account has payment-plan or hold sensitivity?
4. Does the same site or asset show repeated closed SRs without root-cause progression?
5. Is manual closeout effort creating backlog drag that should be surfaced as account workflow friction?

## Cohort interpretation

For Luxottica cohort reviews, aggregate beyond SR-by-SR quality:

- Count closed SRs with inspection findings and no child SR / quote / owner.
- Count weak customer-facing closure notes.
- Separate AP/payment-hold risk from operational completion risk.
- Identify whether backlog is driven by volume, manual Service Channel documentation, missing owner follow-up, or data/system friction.
- Surface the smallest actionable account signal: for example, "inspection findings are not converting to child work," "closure notes do not meet customer-facing evidence requirements," or "invoice workflow is advancing before completion proof."

## What not to do

- Do not infer non-completion from Luxottica name alone.
- Do not mix the payment-plan watch item with completion quality unless the SR is entering invoice/AP workflow or customer hold posture affects payment.
- Do not turn one bad SR into an account-level conclusion unless it carries material customer, payment, safety, or executive-risk implications.
